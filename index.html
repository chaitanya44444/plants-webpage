Types of plants

    Annuals

    Annual plants complete their life cycle within a single growing season and must be replanted every year. Examples include marigolds, zinnias, and petunias.

    African Violet

    African violets are popular indoor plants that are known for their beautiful, purple flowers. They are easy to care for and thrive in bright, indirect sunlight. Water them regularly, but be sure not to overwater as they are susceptible to root rot. African violets also benefit from regular fertilization to promote healthy growth and flowering.

    Perennials

    Perennial plants live for more than two years and typically return to the same location year after year. Examples include daylilies, irises, and hostas.

    Shrubs

    Shrubs are woody plants that are smaller than trees and have multiple stems. Examples include azaleas, hydrangeas, and lilacs.

    Trees

    Trees are large, woody plants that have a single trunk and a canopy of leaves. Examples include oak trees, maple trees, and pine trees.

    Succulents

    Succulents are plants that store water in their leaves, stems, or roots, allowing them to survive in dry environments. Examples include cactus, aloe vera, and jade plants.

    Herbs

    Herbs are plants that are used for their aromatic leaves, seeds, or flowers. Examples include basil, mint, and oregano.

    Vegetables

    Vegetables are plants that are grown and consumed for their edible parts, such as the leaves, stems, roots, or flowers.
